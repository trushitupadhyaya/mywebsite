Brief Description of Research and Consultancy Projects

1. Project Title: Lightning Payload for Venus Orbiter Mission (VOM)

Collaborating Agency: Physical Research Laboratory, Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), Department of Defence, Government of India

Role: Co-Principal Investigator

Status: Ongoing

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 16,00,00,000

Brief Project Description: Lightning isa sudden and violent electrical discharge of short duration that occursin atmosphere of aplanet. It producesoptical signals, ELF and VLFelectromagnetic wavesand acoustic waves. Lightning discharge in Earth’satmosphere occurswhenever electric field exceedsthe breakdown voltage of dry air. On the Venus, Cloudsappearsfrom around 45 km to 70 km. Thecharacterization of Venusian lightning by simulating the Venusian environment in the laboratory conditionsisthe prime objective of the project.

3. Project Title: Design of Planar Antennas for Industrial Applications, 2013

Sponsoring Industry: Twin Engineers, Baroda, Gujarat

Role: Principal Investigator

Status: Completed

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 20,000

Brief Project Description: Planar antenna arrays were developed for 3G mobile communication. The dual-feed dual-polarized dipole array antenna was developed in High Frequency Structure Simulator (HFSS) software simulation, followed by printed circuit board hardware for a client-specific requirement successfully.

4. Project Title: Effect of Electromagnetic Waves on Tissues, 2015-16

Sponsoring Organization: Charotar University of Science and Technology (under i-Club initiative), Changa, Gujarat

Role: Principal Investigator

Status: Completed

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 65,000

Brief Project Description: Electromagnetic waves have shown certain adverse impacts on human tissues. Biophysical and primary biochemical analyses were performed to examine the degradation of plants. Such tests were reported for the first time in literature during the project tenure.

5. Project Title: Design & Implementation of Digital Interface for Active Harmonic Filter Using Hysteresis Current Controller, 2012

Sponsoring Industry: Energy Systems Engineering, Baroda, Gujarat

Role: Principal Investigator

Status: Completed

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 10,000

Brief Project Description: MATLAB code of an active harmonic filter was simulated and developed for the end user. A hysteresis current controller for a 3-phase source was implemented. The client eventually developed the hardware of the controller.

6. Project Title: Solar Cell Maintenance System, Phase-I, 2014-15

Sponsoring Organization: Charotar University of Science & Technology

Role: Principal Investigator

Status: Completed

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 15,000

Brief Project Description: The solar panels were facing efficiency degradation issues due to external dust and moisture particles. The project was developed to clean the solar panel both in manual and automatic mode. In Phase-I of the project, a Real-Time Clock was applied for automatic cleaning operation along with a Zigbee module using Arduino.

7. Project Title: Solar Cell Maintenance System, Phase-II, 2014-15

Sponsoring Organization: Charotar University of Science & Technology

Role: Principal Investigator

Status: Completed

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 40,000

Brief Project Description: After the successful implementation of Phase-I, a sturdy mechanical design for manual and automatic cleaning operation of an array of solar panels was developed for CHARUSAT University. This system was designed to minimize labor efforts in cleaning the solar panel array.

8. Project Title: Dual Band Communication Hub, 2015

Sponsoring Organization: System Level Solutions, Anand, Gujarat, India

Role: Principal Investigator

Status: Completed

Resource Mobilization: Rs. 80,000

Brief Project Description: An onboard antenna solution for 868 MHz resonance was developed with an isolation of 1 MHz frequency. The simulations were carried out in commercial HFSS software. The communication hub incorporated multiple onboard antennas resonating for a mesh network and 2G communication. Despite size constraints, a low-cost, omnidirectional planar patch antenna was successfully developed.
